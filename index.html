Hi, I'm Michelle and I drink lots of water.

Dedicated to helping you stay hydrated, I share the story of my many well-used water bottle friends. 

Just as beautiful flowers need to soak up water, so do you! I hope this page will encourage you to carry around your own personal water bottle friend and drink more of that sweet, life-sustaining substance.

About Me

I love water. I hardly drink anything else. It is just so satiating, and I truly believe it is one of God's greatest gifts to mankind. As a singer, water is so important for keeping my voice healthy, but even if you don't sing, you need water for so many other things going on in your body.

I carry around a water bottle practically everywhere I go because it makes me more likely to drink water if I always have it in my hand. The only issue is that my water bottles are highly susceptible to the various abrasions of daily life. I hope, that in spite of all the silly things that happen to my bottles, my stories will inspire you to carry around one of your own bottles to drink from and create lots of memories with. :)

My Water Bottles

They've been through a lot.
Iconic Blue Dented Water Bottle

Ahh, my beautiful blue water bottle. I had this one throughout three years of high school. Since I carried it everywhere I went, it had many opportunities to be dropped and damaged.
One of the first dents was very soon after I bought it. I dropped from my locker after I filled it, and it hit the linoleum floor quite hard, creating an interesting dent on the bottom edge that allowed it to balance upright while tilted (as pictured). Another time, a boy was putting on his backpack and as he swung his bag, it knocked my bottle off my desk and onto the floor. It landed on the lid, and permanently dented it in a weird sort of tilt. Even when I screwed the lid on, it was tilted, and people would always say,
"Oh! The lid's not screwed on all the way!"
to which I would respond with the tragic story of my blue water bottle.

Hawaii Water Bottle

This next water bottle lived a shorter life span as it had a plastic lid rather than a metal one like the other bottles. I got this one from a Walmart in Hawaii when I went there on my senior trip. (Because what better souvenir to get in Hawaii than a water bottle?) This one lasted my first year of college, but after many bumps and bruises, the top handle was cracked in such a way that I was able to slide hair-ties onto it to store them! That was slightly convenient; however, it was not convenient when I would scratch my hand on the sharp, cracked plastic. Yikes!
The closure for the hole that one drinks out of also became chipped, and it would leak water often...also becoming inconvenient.
So, that bottle is now stored away in the cupboard--no longer holding water, but only memories.

Cool, Color-Changing Water Bottle

This niffy water bottle by Del Sol changes from light to hot pink in the sun! It's the one I am currently using. You can see the cool pattern it creates when I carry the bottle in my crochet net bag. I have to carry it around in a little mesh water-bottle-holder because, as you can see, there is no handle on top like my other bottles. This is not a manufacturing error; it is simply because of the many repeated incidents with this bottle. It's the heaviest one I've had so far, so when it's full to the brim with water, it takes a harder fall--and the concrete is not very forgiving. 
This one probably lasted the longest without getting it's first scratch or dent, but after the handle broke, the number of dents grew exponentially. I was forced to innovate and create my own bottle-carrying-system. Therefore I took to yarn and hook (and some YouTube crochet videos) to create the mesh bag I now carry my beloved pink bottle in.
